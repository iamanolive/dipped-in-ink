what happened when this savage song took the world by storm

i have this bad habit where i read the first chapters of books about 47645 times before moving on to read the rest of it's entirety.

and it's bad because i happen to be an interminable idiot who only does this to the books she likes and makes it her life mission to read every single book whose first-chapter she's ever hated and see it until the end.

i don't know, man. falling in love with books is scary because they take over my life and make me want to read them about a thousand times over and every other book i've ever read seems so dull and worthless in comparison. they make suing the world seem like such a fun prospect BECAUSE THE WORLD IS SO PLAIN AND BORING.

(why can't we have monsters and star-covered girls named ilsa who fall in love with cats)

which is maybe why i run away from stories that promise greatness.

but see here's the real problem. the first time i read this savage song and kate-my-smol-badass burning down a chapel with her lighter and stolen alcohol, i had a copy borrowed from the library. the second time i read this savage song and kate-my-adopted-kid riding cross-country after burning down said chapel and being expelled from school again, i was on vacation and thus a lazy penguin. the third time i read it, i read it all and i didn't know what to say so i sobbed.

it isn't often that anoushka-whose-greatest-pride-in-life-is-talking-people's-ears-off-when-the-talking-in-question-is-about-books-she-loved, reads a book and is left pretty utterly speechless, but this was so nice i don't even know what to say.

schwab is possibly my new favorite person in the world for giving me ilsa and creating august, my smol monster who's more human than most humans i know.

but she's possibly also my least favorite human on earth for making me WANT ILSA SO BAD. how dare you, schwab?? HOW FRICKEN DARE YOU. i shall sue.

BUT THIS WAS AMAZING AND AUGUST FLYNN IS MY KID SO DON'T YOU DARE HURT HIM.

welcome to verity

welcome to verity, the city ruled by monsters who stem from dark dark deeds and feed on humans. there's the malchai (aka pseudo-vampires) who feed on human blood and are catapulted into existence when a human kills someone, and there's the corsai who live in the shadows and survive off of human flesh. after that come the rare sunai, who steal the souls of sinners with their music.

but verity's divided, into a romeo-and-juliet kind of world where humans rule it but the monsters fight for power. the north half of it pays harker-the-evil-human who also happens to be crime boss extraordinaire and keeps monsters like pets in his basement, in return for their protection. the south half fight beside flynn to keep their homes safe. but flynn has the sunai. all of the only three in the world. and he treats them like his children.

it's a very strange little story and it's about existence and surviving in a broken world and it's about not knowing and being afraid and living and it's about a monster named august flynn (my sweet kid) who wants to be human and about a girl named kate harker (my chapel-burning badass) who wants to be a monster. it's also very dark dark dark and violent and i like it very much.

it's about two kids who are essentially enemies but accidentally end up deciding to be friends and betray their families like that. AUGUST IS PRECIOUS AND KATE NEEDS CAKE AND I NEED AN ILSA IN MY LIFE THANKS.
